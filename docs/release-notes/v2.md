# Pantry Run V2 — Household Coordination

> V1.1 made Pantry Run feel safe and re-orderable. V2 makes it feel like
> the place your household actually runs from. We heard you on five
> separate things — categorisation, reminders, todos, push notifications,
> and shared visibility — and this release knocks down the first of them.

---

## What's new

### Smarter categories

The loudest complaint after V1.1 was "most of my items show up under Other."
That was true — the V1 keyword dictionary covered grocery basics but
collapsed on the long tail (haldi, boba pearls, tahini, panipuri masala,
basically anything that wasn't a Coles staple).

V2 categorises in three tiers, in this order:

1. **Your household's own picks win.** Whenever you correct a category in
   the edit sheet (tap any item → Category), Pantry Run remembers that
   choice for the whole household. The next time anyone in your
   household adds the same name, it lands in the right section
   automatically — no LLM call, no waiting.
2. **The expanded keyword dictionary handles ~1,000 common items
   offline.** AU-first spellings (capsicum, coriander, biscuits) with
   US/UK synonyms (bell pepper, cilantro, cookies) and four new
   categories: **Snacks**, **Condiments & Sauces**, **Baby**, and **Pet**.
3. **AI categorisation fills the gap.** For unfamiliar items, Pantry Run
   asks Gemini Flash for a category. The result is cached **globally**
   across all households, so the _next_ household to add "panipuri
   masala" gets an instant hit without any AI call. The more people use
   Pantry Run, the smarter the cache gets.

Offline-added items that fall through the keyword pass land in Other
temporarily and quietly re-categorise themselves on reconnect.

### Notifications — behind-the-scenes plumbing

Settings now has a **Notifications** section where you can turn on push on
each device you use. Nothing actually pings you yet — reminders and task
assignments are the next two milestones — but enabling it now means
you'll be ready the moment those land. iPhone needs the app installed to
the Home Screen first (Apple's rule, not ours); the section explains
what's needed if your browser doesn't support push.

### Coming next in V2

Smarter categories and the push foundation ship first because they
unblock everything else. Still ahead: household reminders ("bin night
every Thursday"), household tasks ("mow the lawn"), an activity feed,
and smart "you usually buy…" suggestions — all rolling out over the
next few weeks.

## Notes

- Your existing items aren't moved. Smarter categories only applies to
  new adds and items you edit. Items already on your list keep their
  current category until you touch them.
- The AI categoriser is rate-limited to 150 calls per household per day.
  If you somehow blow past that, new unknown items just land in Other —
  the rate limit resets at midnight UTC. In practice no household has
  come close.
- Pantry Run still works offline. The keyword dictionary covers most
  common items without any network call, and unknown items repair
  themselves when you come back online.
