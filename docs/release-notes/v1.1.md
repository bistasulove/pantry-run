# Pantry Run V1.1 — Continuity & Trust

> The first big update since V1 launched, shaped by your feedback. The two
> biggest asks were "I'm worried I'll lose my data" and "I retype milk and
> eggs every shopping trip." Both are solved in this release.

---

## What's new

### Save your account

Until now, Pantry Run kept your list tied to one device — no email, no
password, just an anonymous session. Convenient to start, but if you
cleared your browser or got a new phone, your list was gone.

Now you can save your account with an email and password whenever you're
ready. Your household and list stay exactly as they are — nothing moves,
nothing resets. After that, you can sign in on any device (phone,
laptop, tablet) and pick up where you left off. Forgot your password?
There's a reset email.

Find it in **Settings → Save your account**, or wait for the gentle
nudge that appears after about a week of use.

### Quantity and notes

Tap any item in the edit sheet to add a quantity (`2 kg`, `1 dozen`,
`500 mL`) and a note ("the organic one"). Quantities use real units —
grams, kilograms, millilitres, litres, pieces, cans, dozen. The list
stays visually quiet by default: items without a quantity look exactly
as they always did.

### Multiple lists

You can now keep more than one list per household. "Costco Run",
"Weekly Groceries", "Hardware Store" — whatever fits. Tap the list
name at the top of the screen to switch between them. Real-time and
the "● who's here" indicator both rescope cleanly when you switch.

### Staples and Finish Shopping

If you buy milk every week, mark it as a **staple** in the edit sheet.
Then when you tap "Finish shopping" (the renamed "Clear checked"
button), one-off items disappear and your staples stay on the list,
ready for next time. You'll see a preview before you confirm — for
example: "4 items will be removed · 3 staples will stay."

### Shopping history

The new **Clock** tab shows every shopping trip you've finished,
grouped by month, with the finisher's name and item count. Tap any
trip to see what was on it. Forgot to mark something as a staple? You
can restore an item from a past trip with one tap — it lands on your
current list.

## How to update

You don't have to do anything. The next time you open Pantry Run, the
updates are there. If you installed it as an app on your phone, it'll
update on its own in the background.

## Anything off?

If something doesn't feel right, message me. We're still small enough
that every report gets a personal reply.
